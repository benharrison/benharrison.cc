How I Can Serve You

My combined design & engineering background provides a deep and fundamental understanding of many facets of a software project. This has allowed me to shape and develop strong convictions around code cleanliness and structure. One of those convictions is the strategy to create a clean separation between front-end design and back-end functionality code.

From my personal experience the best way to make a project truly user-centric and human-first is to segregate areas of complexity. This means that back-end software contains the lion's share of complexity around functionality, while the front-end tackles complexities in terms of aesthetics, usability, and presentation. My belief is that front-end interfaces should be simple and void of sophisticated software patterns, so that it empowers people who have the appropriate skills to make the best contributions: gifted artists.

Out of this idea I have advocated a loose software architectural pattern that intentionally drives this separation. I call it RAP stack, and you can learn more about it here.

I would like to bring experiences like this, that I have gained, to help setup an organizational structure to build quality products
